  5. pdf A beatiful proof by Hu Ruicheng (Peking University). The problem is that, considering 2 integer sequences defined by
    an+1 = 2an + 3 bn, bn+1 = an + 2 bn, a0=1, b0=0.
    Then one find that an and bn (up to sign) gives all possible the integer solutions of the Pell equation:
    an2-3bn2=1.
    In this note, he gives an elementary proof that the only square integer in {an} is 1. The proof uses a variation of Fermat's infinite descent method, introducing a third argument and do the descent in a roundabout way.
